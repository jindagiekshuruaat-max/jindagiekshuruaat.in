Food Distribution

No one should sleep hungry.

About This Program

Our food distribution program is one of our most consistent and impactful initiatives. Every week, our volunteers gather to prepare and distribute freshly cooked meals to daily-wage workers, homeless individuals, and underprivileged families across Nautanwa and Maharajganj. We also provide dry ration kits — including rice, dal, flour, and oil — to families who need ongoing support.

What We Do
  • Weekly cooked meal distribution at identified locations across Nautanwa
  • Monthly dry ration kits for families living below the poverty line
  • Special food camps during festivals, extreme weather, and emergencies
  • Coordination with local volunteers, donors, and community leaders
  • Hot meal drives during winter months for the homeless
Our Impact So Far

Over 500 meals are distributed every month. More than 200 families receive ration support annually. Since our founding, we have served thousands of individuals across 10+ distribution points.

How to Get Involved

You can volunteer to help cook, pack, or distribute food on our weekly drives. You can also donate groceries or funds — Rs 500 feeds a family for 3 days, and Rs 1,000 covers a full week of meals.

Clothing Support

Warmth and dignity for every person.

About This Program

Clothing is more than fabric — it is dignity. Our clothing support program collects gently used and new clothes from donors across schools, offices, and communities, and distributes them to those who need them most. We run year-round collection drives but intensify our efforts in winter, when the need for warm clothing is most urgent for the homeless and daily-wage workers.

What We Do
  • Year-round clothing collection drives in schools, offices, and communities
  • Special winter blanket and warm clothing distribution camps
  • Targeted support for children, elderly individuals, and daily wage workers
  • Organized distribution camps in underserved villages and localities
  • Sorting and quality checking all donated items before distribution
Our Impact So Far

Over 2,000 pieces of clothing and 300+ blankets have been distributed annually. Our winter drives have reached 500+ individuals in a single season.

How to Get Involved

Donate your gently used clothes, new clothing, or warm blankets. Volunteer at our collection drives or distribution camps. Even Rs 200 can buy a warm pair of socks or gloves for a child.

Girls Safety Awareness

Every girl deserves to feel safe and empowered.

About This Program

Safety is a right, not a privilege. Our Girls Safety Awareness program empowers girls and young women with knowledge about personal safety, their legal rights, and the support systems available to them. Through workshops, street plays, and community discussions, we break the silence around harassment, abuse, and gender inequality — and equip girls with practical tools to protect themselves and speak up.

What We Do
  • Safety awareness workshops in schools, colleges, and community centres
  • Street plays and Nukkad Nataks on girls' rights, consent, and safety
  • Basic self-defense training sessions for young girls and teenagers
  • Helpline awareness drives sharing knowledge of emergency numbers and legal aid
  • Open discussions and sensitization sessions with parents and community leaders
  • Distribution of informational material on girls' rights in local languages
Our Impact So Far

Over 1,500 girls reached through workshops. Campaigns conducted in 15+ schools across Maharajganj. Hundreds of parents sensitized through community engagement sessions.

How to Get Involved

Volunteer as a trainer, counsellor, speaker, or organizer. Support us in reaching more schools and villages. You can also sponsor a workshop for an entire school — contact us to learn how.

Child Labour Prevention

Every child deserves a childhood, not a job.

About This Program

Child labour robs children of their childhood, their education, and their future. Our Child Labour Prevention program works at the grassroots level to identify at-risk families, raise awareness about child labour laws, and connect families with government welfare schemes so they no longer depend on their children's income. We also run school enrollment drives to bring out-of-school children back into classrooms.

What We Do
  • Community awareness campaigns on child labour laws and children's rights
  • Identification and reporting of child labour instances in our area
  • Connecting at-risk families with government welfare schemes and scholarships
  • School enrollment drives to bring out-of-school children back to education
  • Coordination with district administration, police, and local NGOs
  • Follow-up visits to ensure children stay enrolled in school
Our Impact So Far

Helped 100+ children leave exploitative work and re-enroll in school. Awareness campaigns have reached 50+ villages. Families connected with government schemes for sustainable income.

How to Get Involved

Help us identify cases of child labour in your area. Spread awareness in your community. You can also sponsor a child's school supplies, uniform, or fees to help them stay in school.

Plantation Initiatives

Planting trees, planting hope.

About This Program

The environment sustains all life. Our plantation initiative brings together volunteers, schoolchildren, and local communities to plant and nurture trees across Nautanwa and Maharajganj. We focus on native species that support local biodiversity and provide long-term benefits. Crucially, we do not just plant — we follow up for six months to ensure saplings survive and grow into healthy trees.

What We Do
  • Mass plantation drives on World Environment Day, Van Mahotsav, and other occasions
  • School plantation programs with awareness sessions on environmental responsibility
  • Follow-up visits to planted saplings for 6 months post-planting
  • Distribution of saplings to households for home and kitchen gardens
  • Awareness campaigns on deforestation, climate change, and local ecology
  • Partnership with schools, panchayats, and local government bodies
Our Impact So Far

1,000+ trees planted across 20+ locations. Active partnerships with 10+ schools. Dozens of households given saplings to grow at home. Growing green cover in Nautanwa and surrounding areas.

How to Get Involved

Join a plantation drive — no experience needed, just willingness. Sponsor saplings at just Rs 50 each. Adopt a tree and track its growth. Schools can invite us for plantation programs.

Animal Care

Compassion for every living being.

About This Program

A truly compassionate society does not limit its care to humans alone. Our animal care program addresses the suffering of stray and injured animals across Nautanwa. We run daily feeding routes, rescue injured and sick animals, provide first aid and basic treatment, and coordinate with local veterinarians for cases that need professional care. We also run awareness campaigns to promote responsible pet ownership and prevent cruelty.

What We Do
  • Daily feeding routes for stray dogs and cats at 8 locations across Nautanwa
  • Rescue operations for injured, sick, or distressed stray animals
  • First aid and basic medical care for rescued animals
  • Coordination with local veterinarians for treatment and vaccination drives
  • Awareness campaigns on animal rights and responsible pet ownership
  • Anti-cruelty reporting support and community education
Our Impact So Far

10+ animals rescued and treated. Regular feeding maintained at 8 locations. Hundreds of animals fed weekly. Vaccination drives conducted in partnership with local vets.

How to Get Involved

Volunteer on our daily feeding routes. Support our vet costs through donations. Report animals in need to us. Spread awareness about animal welfare in your neighbourhood.
